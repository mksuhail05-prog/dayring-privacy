Privacy Policy — Dayring

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Dayring (“the app”, “we”) is a personal daily planner created by Suhail Mullapullikattil. This policy explains what data the app handles. In short: your data stays on your device. We do not have servers, we do not have accounts, and we do not collect or transmit your personal information.

Information we collect

We do not collect any personal information. Everything you create in Dayring — tasks, workouts, habits, goals, templates, notes/journal, wake & sleep times, and settings — is stored locally on your device only. None of it is sent to us or to any third party. We do not require an account, sign-up, email, or login to use the app.

Health & fitness data (optional)

If you choose to connect Apple Health or Google Health Connect, Dayring reads data such as steps, sleep, and workouts only to display it to you inside the app, on your device. This data is not stored by us, not transmitted, and not shared. You can revoke this access at any time in your device’s Health settings. Dayring never writes to your health records.

Notifications

If you enable reminders or daily nudges, the app schedules local notifications on your device. These are generated on-device and are not sent through any external service.

Data storage, backup & deletion

  • All data is stored locally using on-device storage.
  • The optional “Export data” feature writes a backup file to your device that only you control.
  • Uninstalling the app deletes all of its data from your device.

Children’s privacy

Dayring is a general-purpose planner and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children (and, as stated, we do not collect personal data from anyone).

Third-party services

The current version of Dayring does not use analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with a new “Last updated” date.
