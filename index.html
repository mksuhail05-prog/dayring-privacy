Privacy Policy — Dayring

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Dayring (“the app”, “we”) is a personal daily planner created by Suhail Mullapullikattil. This policy explains what data the app handles. In short: your planning data stays on your device — we run no servers and no accounts. The only third party that may receive limited data is our advertising provider (Google AdMob), described below.

Information we collect

We do not collect personal information ourselves. Everything you create in Dayring — tasks, workouts, habits, goals, templates, notes/journal, wake & sleep times, and settings — is stored locally on your device only. We do not require an account, sign-up, email, or login. (See “Advertising” below for the one exception, which involves our ads provider rather than us.)

Health & fitness data (optional)

If you choose to connect Apple Health or Google Health Connect, Dayring reads data such as steps, sleep, and workouts only to display it to you inside the app, on your device. This data is not stored by us, not transmitted, and not shared. You can revoke this access at any time in your device’s Health settings. Dayring never writes to your health records.

Notifications

If you enable reminders or daily nudges, the app schedules local notifications on your device. These are generated on-device and are not sent through any external service.

Data storage, backup & deletion

  • All data is stored locally using on-device storage.
  • The optional “Export data” feature writes a backup file to your device that only you control.
  • Uninstalling the app deletes all of its data from your device.

Children’s privacy

Dayring is a general-purpose planner and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children (and, as stated, we do not collect personal data from anyone).

Advertising

Dayring may show ads using Google AdMob. To deliver and measure ads, AdMob and its partners may collect and use device information such as an advertising identifier, IP address, and app usage. This data is processed by Google, not by us. Learn more in Google’s advertising policy: policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Where required by law, the app asks for your consent before showing personalized ads. On iOS, ad tracking is also subject to Apple’s App Tracking Transparency permission, which you can change anytime in your device settings. Upgrading to Dayring Pro removes ads.

Other than AdMob, Dayring does not use analytics or tracking SDKs.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with a new “Last updated” date.
